## Standard Definitions

Use these definitions consistently throughout contract documents:

- **"Services"** — The professional consulting services described in the Scope of Services section of this Statement of Work.
- **"Deliverables"** — The tangible work products identified in the Deliverables section, to be provided to Client in the format specified.
- **"Client Materials"** — Information, data, documents, systems access, and other materials provided by Client for use in performing the Services.
- **"Engagement Period"** — The period commencing on the Effective Date and ending on the date specified in the Term section, unless earlier terminated.
- **"Change Order"** — A written amendment to this Statement of Work that documents changes to scope, timeline, or fees, signed by authorized representatives of both parties.
- **"Acceptance Criteria"** — The objective standards against which Deliverables will be evaluated, as specified in the Deliverables section.
- **"Business Day"** — Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays observed by [Firm Name].
- **"Key Personnel"** — The individuals identified in the Team section who are essential to the performance of the Services.

## Scope of Services — Language Patterns

### Inclusive Scope Pattern
> [Firm Name] shall provide the following professional services ("Services") to [Client Name] ("Client") as described in this Statement of Work ("SOW"):

### Phase Description Pattern
> **Phase [N]: [Phase Name]** — During this phase, the [Firm Name] team will [high-level description]. This phase is expected to span approximately [N] weeks, commencing upon [trigger event]. Key activities include:
> - [Activity described using "will" for firm commitments or "may" for discretionary items]

### Exclusion Pattern
> The following items are expressly excluded from the scope of this engagement and, if required, would be addressed through a separate Statement of Work or Change Order:
> - [Exclusion item]

## Deliverables — Language Patterns

### Deliverable Description Pattern
> **Deliverable [ID]: [Name]** — [Firm Name] shall deliver [description] in [format] by [date/milestone]. This deliverable will include [specific contents]. Client acceptance of this deliverable is governed by Section [N] (Acceptance Process).

### Acceptance Process Pattern
> Upon delivery of each Deliverable, Client shall have [N] Business Days to review and either (a) accept the Deliverable in writing, or (b) provide written notice of specific deficiencies. If Client provides notice of deficiencies, [Firm Name] shall have [N] Business Days to address such deficiencies and redeliver. If Client fails to respond within the review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

## Assumptions — Language Patterns

### Standard Assumption Pattern
> This Statement of Work is based upon the following assumptions. Material changes to these assumptions may constitute a scope change and may require a Change Order:
>
> 1. Client shall provide [Firm Name] with timely access to [systems/personnel/data/facilities] as reasonably required to perform the Services.
> 2. Client shall designate a single point of contact ("Client Project Manager") with authority to make day-to-day decisions regarding the Services.
> 3. Client shall make key stakeholders and subject matter experts available for [N] hours per week during [phase/duration].
> 4. [Firm Name] personnel will work remotely unless on-site presence is specifically required, in which case Client shall provide adequate workspace and facilities.

### Impact-if-Wrong Pattern
> If any assumption proves to be materially inaccurate, [Firm Name] reserves the right to propose a Change Order to adjust scope, timeline, or fees to reflect the actual conditions encountered.

## Change Management — Language Patterns

### Change Order Process Pattern
> Either party may propose changes to the scope, timeline, or fees of this SOW. All changes must be documented in a written Change Order that includes:
> - Description of the proposed change
> - Impact on scope, timeline, and fees
> - Approval signatures from authorized representatives of both parties
>
> No change shall be effective until a Change Order has been executed by both parties. Work on changed items shall not commence until the Change Order is approved.

### Standard Confidentiality Pattern

## Termination — Language Patterns

### Termination for Convenience Pattern
> Either party may terminate this SOW for convenience upon [N] days' written notice. Upon termination, Client shall pay [Firm Name] for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination.

### Termination for Cause Pattern
> Either party may terminate this SOW for cause if the other party materially breaches this SOW and fails to cure such breach within [N] days of written notice.

## Usage Notes

- **"Shall" vs. "Will"**: Use "shall" for binding obligations, "will" for descriptions of expected behavior
- **Specificity**: Always quantify where possible (days, hours, dollars) rather than using vague terms
- **Mutuality**: Where possible, make obligations mutual to demonstrate fairness
- **Escape valves**: Always include Change Order provisions so scope adjustments don't require a new contract
- **Client obligations**: Clearly state what the client must provide — this protects against delays caused by client inaction
